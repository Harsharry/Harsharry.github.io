The Beatles Statue

An iconic statue of the Fab Four, in their hometown. The Beatles Statue arrived on Liverpool's Waterfront in December 2015. Donated by the famous Cavern Club, the placement of the statue coincides with the 50 year anniversary of the band's last gig played in Liverpool, at the Liverpool Empire Theatre. It's the city's most popular selfie spot! The figures are larger than life size and weigh 1.2 tonnes in total. They were sculpted by Andrew Edwards who also created the All Together Now Christmas Truce WWI statue.

Opening Hours: 24/7

Buy Tickets: Free!

St Peter's Church, Woolton

×

St Peter's Church, Woolton

St Peter's Church is more than just an active Anglican parish church in Woolton, Liverpool, England. It holds a pivotal place in music history! This very spot is where John Lennon and Paul McCartney met in 1957, a fateful encounter that sparked their legendary songwriting partnership and ultimately led to the formation of The Beatles. The church itself boasts a 90-foot bell tower, making it the highest point in Liverpool. Additionally, beautiful stained glass windows and a grand organ grace the interior.

Paul McCartney's Childhood Home

20 Forthlin Road is the childhood home of Paul McCartney. Owned by the National Trust, this is where Paul lived during the band's formative years. The house itself is a typical example of post-war terraced council housing and offers a glimpse into Paul's early life. It's believed that many of The Beatles' earliest songs were composed and rehearsed here, making it a must-visit for any Beatles enthusiast.

Eleanor Rigby Grave

Eleanor Rigby's Grave

While not directly connected to The Beatles themselves, Eleanor Rigby's grave in the churchyard of St Peter's Church, Woolton, Liverpool, became a legend after inspiring the hauntingly beautiful song of the same name written by Paul McCartney. The song, featured on the Revolver album, tells the story of a lonely life. The identity of the real Eleanor Rigby buried there remains unknown, but the mystery adds to the song's mystique.

Ringo Starr's Childhood Home

This modest house at 10 Admiral Grove in Liverpool, England, was the childhood home of Ringo Starr. Ringo lived here from the age of three until he rose to fame with the band in 1963. The house is now a private residence, but it remains a significant landmark for Beatles fans. It's just down the street from the Sentimental Journey pub and hotel, a popular spot for Beatles enthusiasts.

Brian Epstein's Statue

×

Brian Epstein Statue

This striking bronze statue honors Brian Epstein, the legendary manager of The Beatles. It's located on the corner of Button Street and Whitechapel in Liverpool, England, close to the Epstein family's former NEMS store. The statue depicts Brian striding purposefully along Whitechapel towards Mathew Street, possibly on his way to see The Beatles perform at the Cavern Club for the first time in 1961. In his right hand, he clutches a management contract, symbolizing the pivotal role he played in launching The Beatles' careers.

The statue, unveiled in August 2022, was created by sculptor Andy Edwards, who is also responsible for the Beatles statue on the Liverpool waterfront. It was funded through a public crowdfunding campaign, donations from a generous anonymous donor, and contributions from Liverpool BID Company and The Cavern Club.
